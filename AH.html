Introduction
Maintaining a healthier lifestyle amidst a sea of health advice can be daunting, leaving us uncertain about where to start and how to find reliable interventions.

While independent research is always an option, the barrage of conflicting information can be overwhelming, leading to confusion and inaction.

Knowing Where to Start
20 structured interviews and surveying 31 participants revealed overwhelming advice diversity, skepticism towards recommendations, and lack of social accountability emerged as the top three obstacles to bettering their health.

For many, the challenge was not in knowing what could improve, but rather where to start.

Habits Around Health
Additionally, people mentioned privacy concerns over digital data, struggling to find social accountability, and difficulty navigating an increasingly isolated lifestyle as substantial barriers to health improvement.

Better alternatives exist everywhere but are no match for the distracting sweets, filling fried food, and must-try cocktails.

By the time avoidable outcomes are met, it’s too late. Health is often an afterthought, even for those who have put significant effort into it.
Maze of Advice Culture
Analysis of interview responses revealed a notable trend: individuals often felt “over it” after encountering conflicting advice from various sources.

A couple searches on the web and you’ll find the toxic advice culture everyone and no one seems to be talking about.

Flashy headline after headline. These must-try, get-fit-quick interventions, whose consistent failure to produce the promised results, feeds into a cycle of discouragement.
