On Design
In 1984, Macintosh launched a bold new vision for the computer.

Its symbolic interface and proportional fonts inspired an ease of use revolution that would change computers forever.

It's now the 21st century, and the creative energy of our era, once belonging to fine art, now occupies new technology.

While computers have enabled unimaginable skills and opportunities, in some ways we have lost touch with the cosmic vision of earlier generations.

The ones who contemplated the interconnectedness of the universe and wonder of human existence, who left us their ideas in painting and sculpture.

What is missing from this era, what we all feel is absent, is vision - that hunger to change the world, the foolish optimism it takes to try.
We miss those that saw beyond the material, into worlds far beyond their own. The ones who chose to go to the moon, who just thought differently.

It's then important to remember to think far and stay foolish, so that we get to choose where we are going and create the future we wish to see.

Chance Castaneda
Good Design is Useful
Useful designs fulfill their intended purpose in the most efficient, simple, and straightfoward way.

It does not try to be anything other than what it is. It has true relevance, and finds a snug home in our lives.
Familiar
Good design builds off of interactions and visual language that people are already used to.

Thus, the most familiar and intuitive designs build on people's prior expectations.
