On Design
It's the 21st century, and computers are friendly.

The ease of use revolution led to a sort of cambrian explosion of computation, filling the public psyche with alluring, magical machines.

The creative energy from our era - and likely those after - has drifted from fine art and into new technology.

Highly intricate, personal computers now supply the aesthetic satisfaction once primarily derived from painting and sculpture.

Good design requires acute powers of social observation and futuristic intuition. It demands an independent mind, a willingness to speculate and question.

Without it, we risk sacrificing the cosmic vision of our creative ancestors, whose vast symbol systems contain deep truths about human existence and continue to test our imagination.

Chance Castaneda
Good Design is Useful
Useful designs fulfill their intended purpose in the most efficient, simple, and straightfoward way.

It does not try to be anything other than what it is. It has true relevance, and finds a snug home in our lives.
Familiar
Good design builds off of interactions and visual language that people are already used to.

The most familiar and intuitive designs build on people's prior expectations.
& Magical
