Cosmic Vision
In 1984, Macintosh launched a bold new vision for the computer.

Its symbolic interface and proportional fonts inspired an ease of use revolution, one that saw every computer would soon work this way.

It takes cosmic vision to see so clearly into the future, to know what is not there, what could be there, what has to be there.

Exploration
It's now the 21st century, and the creative energy of our era, once belonging to fine art, mythology, and ideals, now resides with new technology.

What I admire most about our ancestors is their contemplation of the interconnectedness of the universe and wonder of human existence, leaving us their ideas in painting and sculpture.

Their daring exploration to comprehend the world, to map our nature, takes the kind of foolish optimism worth cultivating.
Ambition
People of the 20th century had the choice to stay on their planet, and they chose to go to the moon.

There's something immensely special about engaging in such a challenge, simply for the sake of organizing and measuring the best of our energies and skills.

That trust in human inginuity, in the possibility of excellence, is a clear prerequisite to man's ambition - and it's one I obsessively enforce.
Good Design is Familiar

Useful & Honest
