Explore my particular approach to good design—and get a sense of where I direct my creative energy.

Good Design is Familiar
It feels right in the hand, built to fit into our lives in multiple dimensions.

It considers our prior expectations, standing upon learned interaction and timeless symbolism, archetype, and behavior.
Useful & Honest
It successfully fulfills its purpose in the most efficient, simple, and straightfoward way.

And it does not try to be anything other than what it is.
Magical
It creates an experience that feels insanely smart—with tasteful excess. It may go beyond strict logic, but it always feels instinctively right.
Foolish Optimism
The creative energy of our era, once belonging to fine art, has drifted to new technology.

What's left of our last creative enterprise, inscribed in paintings and sculpture, displays an impulse to create something that lasts.

Of course nothing lasts, but I believe that impulse, that foolish optimism has created the sort of pyramiding achievements that continue to advance our culture.
Trust in Inginuity
The 20th century moon landing stands as a primary influence on my unconscious dreams.

To do what they did with the technology they had put on display a timeless pursuit of imagination.

That trust in human inginuity, in the possibility of dreams, is a clear prerequisite to our ambition—and it's one I obsessively hold true.
