On Design
It's the 21st century, and computers are friendly.

Launched with Macintosh in 1984, the ease of use revolution has made the computer a cornerstone of life.

It promised to be a bicycle to the mind, to be a tool that propels our energies and skills to new heights.

Yet today, I fear we are at risk of becoming more bicycle than mind.

I worry we may conflate the sophistication of our tools with the quality of our lives, our work.

I must dare to question, what if computers have begun to peddle for us, taking us places where if we knew where we were going we wouldn't choose to go?
I take the position that we are always going somewhere - and I hope to aim towards a future where we are without question more mind than bicycle.

Chance Castaneda
Good design is Useful.
Useful designs fulfill their intended purpose in the most efficient, simple, and straightfoward way.

It is honest in that it does not try to be anything other than what it is. It has true relevance, and finds a snug home in our lives.
Familiar.
Good design builds off of interactions and visual language that users are already accustomed to.

The most familiar, and consequently intuitive designs, build upon users prior expectations.
